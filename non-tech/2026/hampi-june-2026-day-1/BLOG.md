Our first day, we arrived sleep deprived in [Hospete](TODO(feniljain): maps link) and took an auto to [Hanumanhalli](TODO(feniljain): maps link), where we stayed in Mani's homestay. It is a minimal 1950s stylish house with owning family living in a small part of the house leaving four rooms open for breakfast.

As we were all tired and it was already hot enough outside, we deicded to explore and take rest in day and climb in evening and night.

After some dosas and paddus, we took onto explore trail of getting upto foot of hills, from where we would climb up to get to various bouldering areas. This path has three sections upto the foot of the hiils. First is a river crossing, we cross Baba boulders before reaching the river, our friend started recalling his experience of leopard sightings, but also assued they are not fond of humans.

It is a pretty small river, with loads of super shiny rocks, they almost look like plastic under sunlight. We cross this section to eneter a small foresty line which is your average foot hill. But after that comes one of my favorite sections, walk thourgh elevated trails amidst paddy fields. There's a super narrow one person path crossing all these fields. In the day one can enjoy the view of these fields push forest around. In the night it becomes an orchestra of toads and crickets, all singing on top of their lungs to created what I call, "Climbers Remix by Nature". (TODO(feniljain): should I post that audio??)

After crossing these fields, we come across a sign showing Hanumanhalli, Hampi, etc. and another saying: (TODO(feniljain): check photo and write down here what was written). After this we take a left and reach a small butterfly heaven. (TODO(feniljain): photo).

I think this was amongst my most favorite places ever!! It had SO SO many different butterflies in a small place completely surrounded by 8-9ft trees. Once we cross this, we reach foot of the hills. For our first day's morning walk, we hiked few minutes up the trail to see a boulder named "double attele". (TODO(feniljain): photos and videos of this boulder, also check if name is correct).

This was our limit for this morning, we turned around and went back to our stay for some good lunch and bus-lagged sleep. Refreshed, everyone gulped coffee at Wanderlust and took the same trail, this time with loads of climbing equipment, and also actually up the hills. I absolutely love hiking up mountains with minimal human interaction. This was the perfect place to scratch that itch, we were walking through lush forest and coming across dozens of small lizards, variety of birds but above all, SO MANY AWESOME BOULDERS!! Hyper was building up as we approached "Party Boulders" area. This was going to be today's climbing destination. It's a set of rocks marked by two primiary boulders in the center with huge illumnati style eyes sprayed on them. (TODO(feniljain): photos you took).

As it was already 5:30 by the time we started, it was close to sunset when we were in the middle of our warmup and I swear to god, this golden hour was one of the most beautiful I have ever experienced. Well after that pleasent distraction we started warming up on nearby boulders. And started our trip's proper routes with a 6a(TODO(feniljain): link grading charts) on the outer line of left party bouder, I was confused on it first, but got he move and feet placement on my 3rd attempt and with that first second of this trip!! Next was another 6a named "Scoop". Aptly named, cause it scooped mine and others asses, even experienced climbers took 10+ attempts to send it and to no-ones surprise I gave on it. With its super spanny start move and high right foot (injured ankle), it just wasn't for me.

While we tried "scoop", it was already dark, so we got our floodlights and headlamps out. This was my first time climbing in the night and complete dark, an hour away from hardest, smallest civilization. Before I would take all that in, I was greeted with my first outdoor project. Its name is "Physics" graded 6b+. On my last Hampi trip, I had flashed a 6b+ called "Psycho Block". (TODO(feniljain): video).

This led me to not think much, it did not seem easy, but also something I couldn't have judged just based on the looks. And man, it didn't disappoint , it absolutely kicked my ass. I couldn't do anything after second move 😭. (TODO(feniljain): video).

I knew it was hard cause even other two experienced climbers with us couldn't complete the full route together. But it was safe to assume they couldn't do it because it was their first day and I know it cause they mentioned they had sent it earlier :P

After 10-15 attempts on it, I didn't have juice left in me, and so was the case with others. So we decided to head back to civilization, we reached back by 10, took showers, had dinner and went to sleep promising to go for early morning climb at 6.
